The Takarazuka Revue

What is the Takarazuka Revue?

The Takarazuka Revue (Takarazuka Kagekidan) is a Japanese theatre company known for being made up entirely of female performers. The actresses are known as Takarasiennes, or siennes, a name taken from the French 'parisienne'. Founded in 1914 by Kobayashi Ichizou, the Revue is made up of two types of actresses:

Musumeyaku, who play female roles, and

Otokoyaku, who play male roles. The Otokoyaku are considered the main draw of the Revue for their elegance and charming masculine performances.

The company is comprised of five official troupes:

Flower Troupe and Moon Troupe, the two original troupes,

Snow Troupe, formed in 1924,

Star Troupe, formed in 1933, and

Cosmos Troupe, formed in 1998.

Each troupe has two star actresses who serve as the face of the troupe, performing in leading roles and representing the company as a whole. They are referred to as the Top Otokoyaku/Top Star and the Top Musumeyaku.
